A City of Opportunity and Education

Tualatin, Oregon, a thriving suburb just south of Portland, is a vibrant community known for its blend of natural beauty, technological innovation, and strong educational focus. With a population of approximately 28,000, Tualatin offers a rich historical legacy and a welcoming environment, making it an ideal place for families and students to thrive. Since 2014, our tutoring service, backed by numerous 5-star Google reviews, has proudly served Tualatin’s residents, providing personalized, affordable academic support for any course or exam, available in-person or online, tailored to meet the needs of this dynamic community.

A Glimpse into Tualatin’s History

Tualatin’s history dates back to 1865, when a small red schoolhouse was built, later becoming Tualatin Elementary and now the site of the Tualatin Food Bank. Named after the Tualatin River, the city was incorporated in 1913 and grew as an agricultural hub, with the Tualatin Plains serving as fertile ground for early settlers. By 1900, a two-room school on Boones Ferry Road marked the start of formal education, with a four-year high school program introduced in 1909. The city’s growth accelerated in the late 20th century, becoming part of Oregon’s “Silicon Forest” with companies like Intel nearby. Today, Tualatin balances its rural heritage with modern development, offering attractions like the Tualatin Commons and the historic Tualatin Heritage Center. Our tutoring service complements this progressive spirit, delivering flexible, high-quality academic support to help Tualatin’s students succeed in a tech-driven world.

The Educational Landscape of Tualatin

Tualatin is primarily served by the Tigard-Tualatin School District (TTSD), a highly rated district with approximately 11,656 students across 17 schools, including 11 elementary schools, three middle schools, three high schools, and a virtual academy, as of the 2024-2025 school year. TTSD is known for its diverse programs, with 47% of students proficient in math and 65% in reading, surpassing state averages of 31% and 44%. Tualatin High School, with 1,747 students, offers advanced programs like International Baccalaureate and a Gifted & Talented program, though it ranks in the bottom 50% of Oregon schools, with 22% math proficiency and 42% reading proficiency. Despite this, its 94% graduation rate exceeds the state’s 81%.

Elementary schools like Bridgeport Elementary, serving 482 students, rank in the top 50% of Oregon schools, with 36% math proficiency and 38% reading proficiency, while Tualatin Elementary, with 396 students, reports 32% and 42%, respectively. Hazelbrook Middle School, with 831 students, also ranks in the top 50%, with 26% math proficiency and 48% reading proficiency. The district’s diversity, with 52% minority enrollment at Tualatin High and 63% at Bridgeport, reflects Tualatin’s inclusive community. Recent reviews praise Tualatin High’s school spirit and resources, though some note challenges with food waste and tardiness policies.

Tualatin’s charter schools, like Multi-Sensory Instruction Teaching Children Hands-on (MITCH), serve smaller groups, with about 100 students in grades K-5, emphasizing agriculture, art, and music. Reviews highlight MITCH’s supportive community but note leadership challenges. Tualatin’s private schools include Horizon Christian School, serving around 150 students, and Stafford Academy, with 48 students in prekindergarten to grade 2, both emphasizing hands-on learning and Christian values. Approximately 20% of Tualatin’s K-12 students attend private schools, higher than the state’s 10% average, with a 90% acceptance rate at private institutions.

Homeschooling in Tualatin: A Growing Option

Homeschooling is on the rise in Tualatin, reflecting Oregon’s robust homeschooling community. In 2024-2025, about 7.03% of Oregon’s K-12 students (roughly 39,000) are homeschooled, up from 6.53% the previous year, with an estimated 22,000 in the Portland metro area, including Tualatin. Nationally, 6.73% of students (3.7 million) are homeschooled, a 51% increase over six years. Oregon’s homeschoolers score at the 71st to 80th percentile on standardized tests, 15-30 points above public school peers. The COVID-19 pandemic drove a 70% surge in homeschooling in 2020-2021, with many families citing concerns about school environments (83%) and instructional quality (41%).

Oregon requires homeschoolers to notify the Northwest Regional Education Service District and administer tests in grades 3, 5, 8, and 10 after an 18-month grace period. Tualatin families access resources like Village Home in Beaverton, offering classes for over 400 learners, and the Tualatin Public Library’s STEM and literacy programs. Microschools and co-ops provide additional support, particularly for students with special needs. Our tutoring service, trusted since 2014 with 5-star Google reviews, supports Tualatin’s homeschoolers with tailored instruction for any subject or exam, ensuring academic success through flexible, affordable in-person or online sessions.
